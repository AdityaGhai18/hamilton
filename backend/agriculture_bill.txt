S. 2024
A BILL
To establish innovative water management solutions and agricultural resilience measures in response to rainfall shortages in the State of Delaware.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the "Delaware Water Resource Management and Agricultural Resilience Act of 2024".
SECTION 2. FINDINGS.
Congress finds that:
(1) The State of Delaware has experienced significant rainfall shortages, threatening agricultural productivity and economic stability;
(2) Traditional water management approaches have proven insufficient to address these challenges;
(3) Innovative solutions are required to ensure sustainable water resources for agricultural and public use.
SECTION 3. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE DELAWARE AGRICULTURAL WATER INNOVATION PROGRAM.
(a) IN GENERAL.—The Secretary of Agriculture shall establish the Delaware Agricultural Water Innovation Program to:
(1) Implement advanced rainfall capture and storage systems;
(2) Develop smart irrigation networks;
(3) Create agricultural water trading markets.
(b) SMART IRRIGATION INFRASTRUCTURE.—
(1) The Secretary shall allocate $150,000,000 for the installation of IoT-enabled irrigation systems that:
(A) Monitor soil moisture in real-time;
(B) Adjust water distribution based on predictive analytics;
(C) Integrate with weather forecasting systems.
SECTION 4. WATER TRADING MARKET PILOT PROGRAM.
(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Secretary shall establish a blockchain-based water-rights trading platform that:
(1) Allows farmers to buy, sell, or lease water rights;
(2) Creates a transparent marketplace for water allocation;
(3) Incentivizes water conservation through market mechanisms.
(b) PARTICIPATION INCENTIVES.—
(1) Tax credits for farmers who:
(A) Achieve verified water savings;
(B) Participate in the water trading market;
(C) Implement approved water conservation technologies.
SECTION 5. FUNDING AND AUTHORIZATION.
(a) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.—There are authorized to be appropriated:
(1) $250,000,000 for fiscal years 2025 through 2029 to carry out this Act;
(2) Such sums as may be necessary for administrative costs.
(b) COST-SHARING REQUIREMENTS.—
(1) The Federal share of any project under this Act shall not exceed 75 percent;
(2) State and private entities shall provide the remaining 25 percent through cash or in-kind contributions.
SECTION 6. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.
(a) ANNUAL REPORT.—The Secretary shall submit to Congress an annual report on:
(1) Program implementation progress;
(2) Water conservation achievements;
(3) Economic impacts on agricultural communities;
(4) Recommendations for program improvements.
